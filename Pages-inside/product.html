Wooden architecture and wood-carving are inextricably linked in the traditional wooden architecture of Sri Lanka, especially in Kandy, the central province of the country. The allied skills simultaneously highlight the construction and carving skills of master-craftsmen and architects; Kandyan wooden architecture is extremely rich in detailing, show-casing a range of wood-carving, much of which represents a remarkable combination of creativity and skill in manipulating the material. Dire Dawa's Foods And Drinks

Ashimi (Sweet Desserts)

sweets are often enjoyed during celebrations, family gatherings, and festive occasions. Made with sugar, spices, and sometimes fragrant ingredients like cinnamon or cardamom, Ashimi adds a warm and joyful touch to local hospitality, showing how Dire Dawa’s diverse traditions come together in its delightful dessert culture.

Fatira

slightly crispy bread is often served warm with honey, sugar, or spiced tea. Whether enjoyed for breakfast or during social gatherings.

Coffee

experience that warms the home, sparks conversation, and celebrates Ethiopia’s legendary coffee heritage.

Tibs

flavorful and popular dish that showcases the city’s love for hearty, spiced meals. Typically made from sautéed meat—beef, lamb, or goat—tossed with onions, peppers, and aromatic spices, Tibs is enjoyed in homes and local eateries alike.

Dire Dawa's Industrial Goods

National Cement Share Company

one of Ethiopia’s leading cement producers, playing a key role in the country’s construction and infrastructure development. With modern production facilities, the company supplies high-quality cement for both residential and large-scale projects, supporting Ethiopia’s growing urbanization and industrial expansion.

Dire Dawa Industrial Park

Strategically located near transport routes, including the Ethio–Djibouti railway, the park hosts a variety of factories producing textiles, garments, and other industrial goods. It provides employment opportunities for thousands of locals and supports the city’s role as a regional trade and investment center, driving industrialization and boosting Dire Dawa’s contribution to Ethiopia’s national economy.

Dire Dawa Food Complex S.C.

It produces a wide range of high‑quality flour‑based products — including wheat flour, pasta and macaroni under the “Vera” brand, biscuits, bread, and animal feed — and has pioneered international food safety and environmental standards in Ethiopia’s food industry. The company supplies its products nationwide, supports local markets with essential foodstuffs, and has built a reputation for quality through certified systems like ISO and FSMS.

Birra coffee

Twholesale and trading company based in Dire Dawa, Ethiopia that deals with coffee products including Harar and other Ethiopian coffees. The firm is involved in the trading and export of coffee beans and related products both domestically and internationally, linking local coffee producers with broader markets.

Dire Dawa's Tradional Spices

Black Cumin(Tikur Azmud)

widely cultivated in eastern Ethiopia, including areas surrounding Dire Dawa.prominent in Dire Dawa, black cumin is often referenced for its healing properties, increasing its cultural value in the region.

Turmeric(Ird)

"As a historic commercial hub linking eastern Ethiopia to export routes, Dire Dawa serves as a vibrant market center where turmeric from other regions is distributed and sold. The golden spice adds rich color and warm flavor to local dishes, and it is also valued in traditional home remedies for its healing properties, making it both a culinary treasure and a symbol of the city’s dynamic trade culture.

Cloves & Cinnamon

spiced tea, traditional dishes, and home remedies, these fragrant spices reflect the city’s historic role as a commercial crossroads, where flavors from different regions blend into everyday life.

Korarima(Ethiopian Cardamom)

cherished for its deep, smoky aroma and rich flavor.Used to season traditional stews, coffee, and spice blends, korarima adds a distinctive Ethiopian character to local cuisine, symbolizing how Dire Dawa brings together flavors from across the country into one vibrant cultural hub.

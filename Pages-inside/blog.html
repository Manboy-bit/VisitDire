Ras Tafari Palace

Sun, December 25th 2025
The Ras Tafari Palace in Dire Dawa, Ethiopia, is a historic landmark built in the early 20th century for Ras Tafari Makonnen, who later became Emperor Haile Selassie I. It stands as a symbol of Ethiopia’s modernization and imperial heritage, reflecting the nation’s early engagement with European architectural styles.

the ancient Harla

Sun, May 25th 2025
The Harla civilization, near Dire Dawa, was an ancient urban society that thrived between the 6th and 15th centuries. Known as the “City of Giants,” Harla is famous for its massive stone ruins, fortified walls, and mosque remains. Skilled in trade, agriculture, and stone construction, the Harla people left behind a mysterious and impressive legacy. Today, their ruins still stand, telling the story of one of the earliest and most remarkable urban centers in the Horn of Africa.

Laga-Oda Cave

Sun, December 25th 2022
Laga-Oda Cave is an archaeological and rock-art site near Dire Dawa in eastern Ethiopia. It contains prehistoric rock paintings depicting animals and human figures, offering insights into the region’s early pastoral and symbolic traditions. The cave is one of Ethiopia’s key sites for understanding ancient art and settlement patterns in the Horn of Africa.
